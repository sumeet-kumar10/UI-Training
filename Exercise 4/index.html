TELEVISION NETWORKS IN INDIA

DOORDHARSHAN

Doordharshan (abbreviated in English as DD) is an autonomous public service provider founded by the goverment of India, owned by the Broadcasting Ministry of India and one of the Prasar Bharti's two division. One of India's largest broadcasting organisations in studio and transmiter infrastructure, it was established on 15 September 1959. Doordharshan which also broadcasts on digital terrestrial transmiter, provides television, radio, online and mobile service throughout metropolitan and regional India and overseas through the Indian Network and Radio India. National telecasts (DD National) were introduced in 1982. Color television begin in India with the live telecast of Independence Day speech by Prime Minister Indira Gandhi on 15 August of that year, followed by the color telecast of the 1982 Asian Games in Delhi. Live telecasts of the opening ceremonies of the 2012 Summer Olympics were broadcast on its national channel, and DD Sports provides round-the-clock coverage.

SONY NETWORKS

Sony Entertainment Television (abbr. SET) is a Indian Hindu-language paid television that was lunched in October 1995, and is owned by Sony Pictures Networks India, a subsidiary of Japanese Sony. SET has been India's most popular television channels, having produced many iconic shows such as CID, Aahat, Indian Idol, Boogie Woogie, Crime Patrol and Beyhadh.

ZEE NETWORKS

Zee Entertainment Enterprises (formerly Zee Teleflims) is an Indian mass media company owned by Essel Group. Headquartered in Mumbai, it has interests in television, print, internet, flim, mobile content and allied businesses. The company was launched on 2 October 1991 as Zee Teleflims, brandname that was retained until 2006. In 2002, the company acquired a majority stake (51%) in ETC Network. In 2006 the acquired Integrated Subscriber Management Services Limited, and in November 2006, it acquired 50% stake in Taj Television, owner of TEN Sports. On that same year the company was rebranded as Zee Entertainment Enterprises.

SUN NETWORKS

Sun TV Network is an Indian mass media company Headquartered in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India. It is a part of Sun Group and is Asia's largest TV network. Established on 14 April 1993 by Kalanithi Maran, it owns a variety of television channel and radio stations in multiple languages. Its flagship channel is Sun TV, which was the first fully privately own tamil channel in India. Sun Group currently owns and operates 32 TV channels (24 SD + 8 HD) across Indian languages - Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Bengali and Marathi. Sun Group will be launching 7 new HD channels and 3 new SD channels including network's first channels in Marathi language making the entry of the company into North Indian market after sun bangla. After this sun group will have a total of 42 channels in their bouquet.

STAR NETWORKS

Star India Private Limited is an Indian media conglomerate and a wholly owned subsidiary of the Walt Disney Company India. It is headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra. It has a network of 60 channels in eight different languages, reaching out to 9 out of 10 cable and satellite TV homes in India. The network reaches approximately 790 million viewers a month across India and more than 100 countries. Star India generates more than 30,000 hours of content every year.
